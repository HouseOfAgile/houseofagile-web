## The Challenge

Universal Music Group - one of the world's three major music labels - needed to overhaul its global music metadata systems. These systems were the foundation for everything: digital rights management, royalty calculations, and content delivery for a catalog spanning millions of tracks.

The technical challenge was significant, but the organizational challenge was harder: **coordinating 120+ engineers across 5 cross-functional teams**, spanning multiple countries, time zones, and vendor relationships - all while keeping existing business operations running.

## Our Role

Served as **Project Delivery Manager and Architecture Head**, responsible for both the technical architecture and the delivery organization:

- **Architecture**: Designed the SOA integration backbone and enterprise service bus that connected rights management, digital asset management, and content delivery
- **Standards**: Defined unified metadata schemas that enabled consistent data exchange across previously siloed systems
- **Team leadership**: Built and coordinated the 5 delivery teams, implemented Agile practices across the organization
- **Vendor management**: Integrated external development partners into a single delivery rhythm

## Results & Impact

The platform delivered a globally scalable foundation for UMG's digital operations during the most critical transition in music industry history - from physical to digital distribution. Coordinating 120+ engineers over three years without major delivery failure validated the development factory model. The Agile practices introduced during the program became the engineering organization's standard way of working.

**Why this matters for our clients**: This engagement demonstrates our ability to lead large, complex technical organizations - not just write code. The skills that made this work (architecture thinking, team coordination, vendor management, delivery discipline) are the same ones we bring to smaller-scale engagements today.
